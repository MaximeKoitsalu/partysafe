# Disclaimer

**partysafe is information, not medical advice.**

This site exists to help people make informed decisions about substances they
have already chosen to use. It does not encourage use; it does not endorse any
substance; it does not replace the judgment of a physician, pharmacist, or
emergency medical professional.

## What this site does

- Shows reported combination risks between substances based on the TripSit
  community-maintained dataset
- Explains the pharmacological reasoning behind each risk in plain English
- Documents typical onset, peak, and duration windows
- Lists warning signs and first-aid steps reviewed by clinicians (see ATTRIBUTION.md)
- Provides emergency hotline numbers for multiple regions

## What this site does NOT do

- Provide personalized medical advice
- Account for your specific medical history, prescriptions, weight, age, body
  chemistry, mental state, or any other individual factors
- Guarantee accuracy. Combo data is community-maintained and may contain errors.
  We document known disputes (see "Reviewed — disputed" annotations in the UI).
- Endorse, promote, or recommend the use of any substance
- Address interactions with prescription medications (consult a pharmacist)

## In an emergency

**Call emergency services immediately.**

  - United States: **911**
  - United Kingdom: **999**
  - European Union: **112**
  - Other regions: see /emergency in the app

**United States — Never Use Alone hotline: 1-800-484-3731**

If someone is unresponsive, has slow or stopped breathing, has blue lips or
fingertips, is making gurgling or choking sounds, or feels cold and clammy,
they may be experiencing an overdose. Place them in the recovery position
(on their side, top knee bent, head tilted back so vomit can drain), stay with
them, and call emergency services.
